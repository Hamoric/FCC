Jaswant Singh Khalra

Here is the timeline of Bhai Jaswant Singh Khalra's life:

  • Born in Khalra, Tarn Taran, India in 1952
  • Worked as the director of a bank in Amristar throughout the 1980s where punjab was undergoing a genocide and there was widespread abuses of power and police brutality in the whole state
  • Began investigating when several of his Sikh collegues went missing and further investigation revealed the existence of files from the Amritsar municipal government which gave the names, ages and addresses of a number of people who had been killed in police custody in Amritsar
  • Discovered that the Punjab police had been accusing young Sikhs of being militants and killing them in staged shootings, or else disappearing them while they were in custody and illegally cremating the bodies to cover it up
  • Uncovered that 25,000 unlawful cremations had taken place in Punjab and that the victims included approximately 2,000 police officers who had been refusing to go along with the cover-up
  • Released the discoveries in early 1995 with help from the National Human Rights Commission, sparking worldwide outrage.
  • Soon after, he announced at a press conference that he expected to be killed and was prepared to die in the cause of justice, but that the Indian government should not think that his death would make the matter of 25, 000 murders go away
  • Abducted by Punjab Police while washing his car in front of his house on September 6, 1995
  • Six Punjab Police Officials conicted in 2005 out of which two recieved life sentences and four recieved seven years improsonment which was extended to life improsonment in 2007
